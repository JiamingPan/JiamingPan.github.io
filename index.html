About

I'm a Physics PhD candidate at the University of Michigan, graduating in 2027. My research extracts weak statistical signals from noisy, high-dimensional data — measuring small cosmological effects across surveys of tens of millions of galaxies — and builds the scalable inference pipelines that make those measurements reproducible.

The work runs on Bayesian inference, uncertainty quantification, model validation, and numerical optimization, executed on high-performance computing clusters. I treat robustness as a first-class concern: whether a result holds up under systematic error, alternative modeling assumptions, and out-of-sample data.

More recently I've worked on machine learning directly — LLM compression and optimization, and diffusion models for scientific simulation data — with a focus on generative modeling under limited data and validation beyond surface-level sample quality. I'm looking for full-time roles in machine learning, AI research, quantitative research, and quantitative modeling.

LLM Compression & Optimization

May 2026 – Present

Machine Learning Research Intern · Escha Labs

Tackled the weight-reconstruction step of large language model compression, where quantized weights must be recovered accurately without inflating inference cost. Modeled the distribution of quantized weight codes and found that a simple Laplace model matches the empirical histograms to roughly 2.05–2.15 bits per symbol, then designed and benchmarked batched solvers for the underlying binary least-squares problem — exact search, iterative local search, and GPU candidate search — across accuracy and runtime regimes. Tested on quantized Qwen3-8B weights; solver diagnostics and method selection fed a downstream reconstruction pipeline that reached a 14× speedup over the prior baseline. Separately built a sensitivity framework to identify "super weights" — a small set of unusually load-bearing parameters whose removal sharply degrades the model — and to separate them from outlier weights that can be safely removed or corrected.

Stack: Python · PyTorch · CUDA · NumPy

Diffusion Models for Scientific Simulation Data

Generative modeling of scientific fields is hard to evaluate: training data is limited and visual sample quality is a misleading proxy for correctness. Built a physics-aware evaluation and reproducibility framework for diffusion models trained on CAMELS 128³ cosmological simulation fields — developing the data pipeline that converts raw 3D fields into trainable 2D slices and running controlled ablations on normalization, augmentation, noise schedules, and EMA. The core contribution is scientific validation: radial power-spectrum ratios, field one-point statistics, nearest-neighbor distance tests for memorization, and PCA feature-space comparisons, used together to separate genuine generalization from memorization and to flag sample-quality failures and unstable training. Base diffusion training builds on the open-source cosmo_diffusion package.

Stack: Python · PyTorch · NumPy · Jupyter

Large-Scale Bayesian Inference Pipeline

The recurring problem in survey cosmology is also a quantitative one: extracting weak statistical signals from tens of millions of noisy, high-dimensional observations. Built scalable Python and JAX pipelines for the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) and automated large-scale MCMC inference on high-performance computing clusters — batch execution, convergence diagnostics, result aggregation, and reproducible comparison across competing models. Designed the validation layer around robustness: sensitivity analysis, systematic-bias tests, and stress tests that surface fragile modeling assumptions before they reach a result. The outcome is faster, auditable model testing on data where the signal is small relative to the noise.

Stack: Python · JAX · NumPy · SciPy · Slurm · HPC

Research

My doctoral research is in theoretical and observational cosmology — dark energy, modified gravity, and large-scale structure — as a member of the DESI collaboration. In practice it is quantitative work: statistical inference on large-scale survey data, comparison of competing models, uncertainty quantification, and robustness to systematic error. The peer-reviewed papers below are the output of that work.

21 papers · 5,000+ citations · h-index 16  —  Google Scholar · InspireHEP

Modified Gravity Constraints with DESI

Constraints on Horndeski and non-minimally coupled gravity from DESI DR2 and Y1 data, including work on the EFT of Dark Energy analysis pipeline, scale dependence in full-shape clustering, and cosmological parameter estimation.

J. Pan and G. Ye, Non-minimally Coupled Gravity Constraints from DESI DR2 Data (2025), arXiv:2503.19898.

M. Ishak, J. Pan, R. Calderon et al., Modified gravity constraints from full-shape modeling of clustering measurements from DESI 2024, J. Cosmology Astropart. Phys. 09 (2025) 053, arXiv:2411.12026.

Hubble Constant from Galaxy–GW Cross-Correlations

A tomographic cross-correlation pipeline using galaxy surveys and gravitational-wave events to constrain H₀ without host-galaxy identification, with Fisher forecasts quantifying how uncertainty scales with event count and localization accuracy.

J. Pan, D. Huterer, C. Avestruz et al., Determining the Hubble Constant through Cross-Correlation of Galaxies and Gravitational Waves, Phys. Rev. D 113, 103532 (2026), arXiv:2510.19931.

Consistent Initial Conditions for Early Modified Gravity

Derived consistent adiabatic initial conditions for Horndeski gravity within the EFT framework and implemented them in cosmological Boltzmann codes, resolving long-standing inconsistencies in early-time evolution.

J. Pan, M.-X. Lin, G. Ye, M. Raveri and A. Silvestri, Consistent Initial Conditions for Early Modified Gravity in Effective Field Theory, Phys. Rev. D 112 (2025) 083551, arXiv:2506.17411.

Robustness of BAO Analysis in Modified Gravity

Showed that the standard baryon acoustic oscillation analysis remains robust in Horndeski models, with negligible bias in BAO compression when applied to modified-gravity data.

J. Pan, D. Huterer, F. Andrade-Oliveira and C. Avestruz, Compressed baryon acoustic oscillation analysis is robust to modified-gravity models, J. Cosmology Astropart. Phys. (2024) 051, arXiv:2312.05177.
