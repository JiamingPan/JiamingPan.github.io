About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Physics Department at the University of Michigan, working in theoretical cosmology. My research focuses on the physics of cosmic acceleration, combining model building, large-scale structure analysis, and statistical methods to test the nature of dark energy and gravity. My advisors are Prof. Dragan Huterer and Prof. Camille Avestruz.

I received my bachelor's degree from the University of Michigan with majors in Physics and Astrophysics and a minor in Philosophy. I am broadly motivated by the fundamental questions of why the universe exists, how it evolves, and what physical laws govern its largest scales. My work spans both theoretical frameworks and observational probes, with the goal of understanding the origin and dynamics of cosmic acceleration.

Research

Current Work

I work at the interface of cosmology, statistics, and large-scale data analysis. I am an active member of the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) collaboration, where I build and run analysis pipelines for large-scale structure, perform cosmological parameter estimation, and test extensions of ΛCDM such as evolving dark energy and modified gravity. See my publications at https://inspirehep.net/authors/2605724?ui-citation-summary=true.

My publications have received 3800+ citations and I currently have an h-index of 16.

Modified Gravity Constraints with DESI

I work on testing extensions to General Relativity using large-scale structure data from the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI). My recent work includes deriving constraints on Horndeski and non-minimally coupled gravity models using DESI DR2 and Y1 data. I helped build and run the EFT of Dark Energy analysis pipeline, explored scale dependence in full-shape clustering, and contributed to cosmological parameter estimation. These analyses provide high-precision limits on modifications to gravity at linear and quasi-linear scales.

J. PAN and G. Ye, Non-minimally Coupled Gravity Constraints from DESI DR2 Data (2025), arXiv:2503.19898.
M. Ishak, Pan, Jiaming*, R. Calderon et al., Modified gravity constraints from the full shape modeling of clustering measurements from DESI 2024, J. Cosmology Astropart. Phys. 09 (2025) 053, arXiv:2411.12026.

Determining the Hubble Constant with Galaxy–GW Cross-Correlations

I developed a tomographic cross-correlation pipeline that uses galaxy survey and gravitational- ave events to constrain the Hubble constant without relying on host-galaxy identification. I performed Fisher forecasts, quantified how the uncertainty scales with the number of events and their localization errors.

J. PAN, D. Huterer, C. Avestruz, D. H. T. Cheung, E. Trott, N. Dalal et al., Determining the Hubble Constant through Cross-Correlation of Galaxies and Gravitational Waves (2025), arXiv:2510.19931, under revision in Phys. Rev. D.

Initial Conditions for Horndeski Gravity

I derived consistent adiabatic initial conditions for Horndeski gravity within the Effective Field Theory (EFT) framework and implemented them in cosmological Boltzmann codes. This work resolves long-standing inconsistencies in early-time evolution and enables accurate predictions for CMB and large-scale structure observables in modified-gravity models. I carried out both the theoretical derivation and the numerical validation.

J. PAN, M.-X. Lin, G. Ye, M. Raveri and A. Silvestri, Consistent Initial Conditions for Early Modified Gravity in Effective Field Theory, Phys. Rev. D 112 (2025) 083551, arXiv:2506.17411.

BAO Analysis in Modified-Gravity Models

I have studied the robustness of the standard baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO) analysis in the modified-gravity (Horndeski) models. Our findings showed negligible bias in the BAO compression analysis when applied to data in the modified-gravity models.

J. PAN, D. Huterer, F. Andrade-Oliveira and C. Avestruz, Compressed baryon acoustic oscillation analysis is robust to modified-gravity models, J. Cosmology Astropart. Phys. (2024) 051 [2312.05177].

Star Clusters

I have worked on star clusters with Prof. Eric Bell. We've investigated the M81 globular cluster system. We found evidence for coherent velocity structure in its system, with a number of clusters clearly being associated with the accretion of M82 into the M81 group.

J. PAN, E. F. Bell, A. Smercina, P. Price, C. T. Slater, J. Bailin et al., New globular cluster candidates in the M81 group, MNRAS 515 (2022) 48 [2206.07251].

Solar System Objects

I've worked with Prof. David Gerdes and Dr. Ed Lin in the study of Jupiter Trojans. We found Trojans' colors become less red with fainter absolute magnitudes in the Dark Energy Survey data, further supporting the color-size correlation.

J. PAN, H. W. Lin, D. W. Gerdes, K. J. Napier, J. Wang, T. M. C. Abbott et al., Photometric Properties of Jupiter Trojans Detected by the Dark Energy Survey, PSJ 3 (2022) 269.
